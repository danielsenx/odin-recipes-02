Butter Chicken with Basmati Rice

Home

Description

Indian Butter Chicken, or Murgh Makhani, is a rich and creamy tomato-based curry made with tender pieces of chicken simmered in a buttery, spiced sauce. This dish is a classic in Indian cuisine, known for its balance of savory, slightly sweet, and mildly spicy flavors. Served over aromatic basmati rice, it’s a comforting and satisfying meal perfect for any day of the week.

Ingredients:

  • For the Chicken Marinade:
    • 1.5 lbs (700g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
    • 1 cup plain yogurt
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste (or 1 tsp each of minced ginger and garlic)
    • Spices:
      • 1 teaspoon garam masala
      • 1 teaspoon turmeric
      • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
      • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
      • 1 teaspoon salt
  • For the Butter Chicken Sauce:
    • 2 tablespoons butter
    • 1 tablespoon oil (optional, to prevent butter from burning)
    • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
    • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    • Spices:
      • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
      • 1 teaspoon paprika
      • 1 teaspoon garam masala
      • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
      • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (optional)
    • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes or tomato puree
    • 1 cup heavy cream or full-fat coconut milk
    • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, balances acidity)
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh cilantro (coriander), chopped, for garnish
  • For the Rice:
    • 1.5 cups basmati rice
    • 3 cups water
    • 1 tablespoon butter or oil
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1-2 whole cloves, 1 bay leaf (optional, for aroma)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    1. In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, and spices. Add the chicken and coat well.
    2. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight for best flavor.
  2. Cook the Chicken:
    1. Heat a large pan or skillet over medium-high heat. Add a little oil and cook the marinated chicken in batches until browned on all sides (it doesn't need to be fully cooked). Set aside.
  3. Make the Sauce:
    1. In the same pan, add butter (and a bit of oil if needed). Sauté the chopped onion until soft and golden.
    2. Stir in ginger-garlic paste, cumin, paprika, garam masala, cinnamon, and chili powder. Cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
    3. Add the crushed tomatoes and simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    4. Blend the sauce (optional) using an immersion blender or regular blender for a smooth texture.
    5. Return the sauce to the pan, stir in the cream and sugar, and season with salt to taste.
    6. Add the browned chicken back to the pan and simmer gently for 10-15 minutes, until the chicken is fully cooked and the sauce thickens.
  4. Cook the Rice:
    1. Rinse basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    2. In a pot, add rice, water, butter, salt, and optional whole spices.
    3. Bring to a boil, reduce to a simmer, cover, and cook for 15 minutes or until water is absorbed and rice is tender.
    4. Let rest 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork.
  5. Serve:
    1. Spoon the butter chicken over the rice.
    2. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve with naan or roti if desired.
