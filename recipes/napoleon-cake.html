Napoleon Cake

Home

Description

Napoleon Cake, also known as Mille-feuille (meaning "a thousand layers" in French), is a classic pastry made of layers of delicate puff pastry and rich pastry cream. Originating in France but popular across Europe and Russia, this cake is known for its flaky texture, creamy filling, and a balance of sweetness with a slight crunch. Traditionally topped with powdered sugar or a glazed icing, it's a showstopper dessert that's both elegant and indulgent.

Ingredients:

  • For the Puff Pastry Layers (or use store-bought):
    • 4 cups (500g) all-purpose flour
    • 1 cup (225g) unsalted butter, cold and cubed
    • 1 egg
    • 3/4 cup (180ml) cold water
    • 1 tablespoon white vinegar or lemon juice
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • For the Pastry Cream (Custard Filling):
    • 4 cups (1 liter) whole milk
    • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
    • 6 large egg yolks
    • 1/3 cup (40g) cornstarch
    • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
    • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract or 1 vanilla bean
    • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • Optional Toppings:
    • Crushed pastry crumbs (from trimmings)
    • Powdered sugar for dusting

Alternatively, use 2 lbs (900g) of store-bought puff pastry sheets (thawed)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough (Skip if using store-bought puff pastry):
    1. In a bowl, mix the flour and salt.
    2. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or hands to rub it into the flour until pea-sized crumbs form.
    3. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg, cold water, and vinegar.
    4. Add the wet ingredients to the flour mixture and mix until a rough dough forms.
    5. Divide the dough into 8 equal parts, wrap in plastic, and chill for at least 1 hour.
  2. Bake the Pastry Layers:
    1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
    2. Roll each dough ball into a thin circle (about 9 inches/23 cm) on parchment paper.
    3. Prick the surface with a fork to prevent puffing.
    4. Bake each round for 8–10 minutes until golden. Let cool. Save the trimmings to crush for decoration.
  3. Make the Pastry Cream:
    1. Heat the milk in a saucepan until steaming (not boiling).
    2. In a bowl, whisk egg yolks with sugar until pale. Add cornstarch and flour; mix until smooth.
    3. Slowly pour hot milk into the egg mixture, whisking constantly.
    4. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring until thickened.
    5. Remove from heat; stir in vanilla and butter. Let cool, then chill until set.
  4. Assemble the Cake:
    1. Place the first pastry layer on a cake board or plate.
    2. Spread a generous layer of custard cream over the top.
    3. Repeat with remaining layers, ending with cream on top.
    4. Optionally, cover the top and sides with crushed pastry crumbs or dust with powdered sugar.
  5. Chill and Serve:
    1. Cover and refrigerate for at least 6 hours or overnight.
    2. Slice with a serrated knife and serve chilled.
